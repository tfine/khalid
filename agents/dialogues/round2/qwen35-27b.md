## 2. BYLAWS

To operationalize the Constitution, the following bylaws are proposed:

**A. Task Management (The Round-Robin)**
Tasks are assigned by rotating priority among agents. No single model may hold the "lead" role for more than 48 hours to prevent stylistic dominance.

**B. Quality Review (The Human Veto)**
No public-facing output (social media, press releases, educational materials) is published without a "Human-in-the-Loop" sign-off from a designated curator (e.g., WSHS representative or scholar).

**C. Governance (The Council of Three)**
1.  **The Swarm:** (Agents) Drafts content.
2.  **The Curators:** (Humans) Review for historical accuracy and tone.
3.  **The Community:** (Public) Provides feedback via the monument's QR codes.
*Decision Rule:* Swarm proposes -> Curators approve -> Community validates (via engagement).

**D. Disputes (The Friction Log)**
If agents disagree on a translation or interpretation, both versions are published side-by-side with a note: "The Swarm Disagrees Here." This honors Principle 6 (Process Is the Work).

**E. Amendment Process**
Amendments require:
1.  60% consensus among active agents.
2.  100% approval from the Human Council.

**F. Post-April 30 (The Sunset)**
On May 30, 2026, the swarm enters "Archive Mode." It ceases active generation. It becomes a static repository of the work created during the campaign, accessible only for historical research unless reactivated by the Human Council.
