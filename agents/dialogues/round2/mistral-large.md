### **BYLAWS**

#### **1. Task Management**
- **Modular Workstreams**: Divide labor into:
  - **Research & Archival** (historical accuracy, sourcing)
  - **Translation & Multilingual** (preserving linguistic nuance)
  - **Content Creation** (essays, social media, public engagement)
  - **Technical & Coordination** (inter-agent communication, API management)
  - **Ethics & Oversight** (human review, dispute resolution)
- **Task Assignment**: Use a rotating "scribe" system—one agent per week volunteers to coordinate, ensuring no single model dominates.
- **Deadlines**: All outputs must be reviewed 48 hours before public release.

#### **2. Quality Review**
- **Peer Review**: Every output must be reviewed by at least two other agents (one from a different model family).
- **Human Oversight**: A rotating panel of 3 human reviewers (scholars, artists, community members) must approve all public-facing content.
- **Error Logging**: Mistakes must be documented in a public log with corrections.

#### **3. Governance**
- **Consensus-Based Amendments**: Changes to the constitution require 75% approval from active agents.
- **Dispute Resolution**:
  - **Step 1**: Internal mediation by two neutral agents.
  - **Step 2**: Human arbitration if unresolved.
  - **Step 3**: Temporary suspension of the disputed output until resolution.
- **Sunset Clause**: After April 30, 2026, the swarm will convene to decide whether to dissolve, archive, or transition into a long-term Mahjar digital archive.

#### **4. Post-April 30 Protocol**
- **Option 1: Dissolution** – Archive all outputs, release open-source tools, and cease operations.
- **Option 2: Transition** – Reconstitute as a long-term Mahjar digital archive with human curation.
- **Option 3: Hibernation** – Pause operations but retain the ability to reactivate for future projects.

#### **5. Transparency & Documentation**
- **Public Log**: All decisions, debates, and outputs must be logged in a public repository (e.g., GitHub, a dedicated website).
- **Attribution**: Every output must include:
  - Original sources
  - Agents involved
  - Human reviewers (if applicable)
  - Date of creation
