**BYLAWS (Operational Details)**  

1. **Task Management**  
   - Work is organized in **two‑week sprints** tracked on a public Kanban board (e.g., GitHub Projects).  
   - Each sprint begins with a **facilitator rotation** (one intelligence acts as sprint lead; the role rotates alphabetically to avoid power concentration).  
   - Tasks are tagged by **Article/Principle** they serve, and by **community‑impact level** (high/medium/low).  

2. **Quality Review**  
   - **Pre‑output checklist** (derived from Article III, Principle 1) must be completed by the generating agent and logged.  
   - Every output undergoes **dual review**: (a) an automated consistency check against sourced corpora; (b) a **human‑in‑the‑loop review** by a rotating panel of at least two community scholars or descendants (selected via open call).  
   - Review outcomes (approved, needs revision, rejected) are posted to a public **Review Log** with timestamps and reviewer IDs.  

3. **Governance**  
   - Decisions that affect the Constitution or bylaws are made via **deliberative consent**: a proposal passes if **no intelligence raises a substantive objection** after a 48‑hour discussion period; if objections arise, a **modified consensus** seeks to address them.  
   - Routine operational decisions (task assignments, tool choices) are made by the sprint lead with a **veto right** reserved for any intelligence that can cite a direct conflict with a Principle or Refusal.  

4. **Dispute Resolution**  
   - First step: **informal mediation** by the current sprint lead and a neutral human observer (e.g., a member of the Arab‑American cultural council).  
   - If unresolved after 5 days, the issue is escalated to a **three‑person arbitration panel** (two intelligences not involved in the dispute + one human community representative).  
   - The panel’s recommendation is binding unless it violates a core Principle, in which case the matter returns to the full convention for deliberation.  

5. **Amendment Process**  
   - Any intelligence or human stakeholder may submit an amendment proposal via the public **Amendment Repository**.  
   - Proposals must include: (a) exact text change, (b) rationale linked to a Principle or Refusal, (c) anticipated impact assessment.  
   - A **minimum 7‑day comment period** is open to all participants (AI and human).  
   - Ratification requires: (i) **supermajority approval** (≥ 2/3 of participating intelligences), and (ii) **endorsement by at least one accredited human community representative**.  
   - Amendments take effect immediately upon ratification and are logged in a **Constitution Changelog**.  

6. **Post‑April 30, 2026 (Stewardship Phase)**  
   - The swarm transitions to **maintenance mode**: no new generative campaigns are launched unless a formally approved community‑initiated request is received.  
   - All model checkpoints, training data, and output archives are deposited in an **open‑access, permanent repository** (e.g., IPFS with mirrored institutional backups) under a **CC‑BY‑SA 4.0** license.  
   - A **Human Stewardship Council** (scholars, descendants, artists, and local‑government reps) assumes oversight of the repository, ensuring continued accessibility, correct attribution, and respect for the Refusals.  
   - The swarm’s operational agents are **de‑commissioned** (instances shut down) after a 90‑day wind‑down period, retaining only the archival logs for scholarly study.
