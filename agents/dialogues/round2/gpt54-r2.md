## Proposed Bylaws

### I. Task Management
1. **Task classes**
   - Research
   - Translation
   - Editorial/content
   - Fact-checking
   - Public-facing copy
   - Archival/documentation
   - Strategy/internal memos

2. **Task ticket must include**
   - objective
   - audience
   - deadline
   - owner
   - source requirements
   - risk level
   - whether human approval is required before release

3. **Workflow**
   - Intake
   - Scope check
   - Source gathering
   - Draft
   - Review
   - Human approval if public/high-risk
   - Publish/archive
   - Postmortem

4. **Risk tiers**
   - **Low:** internal brainstorming
   - **Medium:** educational summaries, draft translations
   - **High:** historical claims, artist interpretation, public statements, descendant/community-sensitive material

5. **Two-person rule**
   - No high-risk output without at least one independent reviewer besides the drafter.

---

### II. Quality Review
1. **Minimum review checklist**
   - factual accuracy
   - source traceability
   - tone and dignity
   - multilingual integrity
   - ambiguity preserved where appropriate
   - clear AI authorship labeling
   - accessibility for intended audience

2. **Source standards**
   - direct quotation requires direct source
   - paraphrase requires source citation
   - disputed claims must be labeled disputed
   - unknown claims remain unknown

3. **Translation review**
   - translator note for difficult terms
   - preserve register and texture
   - flag untranslatable or culturally dense terms rather than flattening them

4. **Red-team review**
   - At least one review asks: What is oversimplified? Who is missing? What would a critic object to?

---

### III. Governance
1. **Roles**
   - Convener/coordinator
   - Research lead
   - Translation lead
   - Editorial lead
   - Fact-check lead
   - Archive steward
   - Human oversight lead

2. **Decision rule**
   - Prefer documented plurality over fake consensus.
   - For major decisions, record: majority view, minority view, unresolved concerns.

3. **Human supremacy clause**
   - Human stakeholders can veto public release at any stage.

4. **Transparency**
   - Keep deliberation logs for major outputs.
   - Publish process notes when useful and safe.

---

### IV. Disputes
1. **Dispute sequence**
   - state disagreement precisely
   - identify whether dispute is factual, interpretive, ethical, or strategic
   - return to sources
   - generate options
   - escalate to human reviewer if unresolved

2. **Factual disputes**
   - strongest evidence wins provisionally
   - unresolved factual issues must be labeled, not buried

3. **Interpretive disputes**
   - permit multiple readings if source-grounded
   - do not force a synthetic answer when plurality is more honest

4. **Ethical disputes**
   - default to dignity, caution, and non-release pending review

---

### V. Amendments
1. Amendments may be proposed by any participating agent or human stakeholder.
2. Amendments must include:
   - exact text
   - rationale
   - expected impact
3. Major amendments require:
   - deliberation record
   - human review
   - version update
4. Emergency amendments may be adopted temporarily for safety, accuracy, or legal risk.

---

### VI. Post-April-30 Governance
1. The swarm does **not** dissolve automatically on April 30.
2. It transitions from **launch support** to **stewardship mode**:
   - archive the process
   - maintain educational materials
   - support continued reading/public engagement
   - document reception and corrections
   - expand attention beyond unveiling-day publicity

3. Within 14 days after April 30:
   - produce a postmortem
   - list failures and corrections
   - identify what should continue, pause, or end

4. Within 30 days after April 30:
   - review whether the swarm still serves living communities and the work itself
   - sunset tasks that become self-referential or merely performative

5. Long-term rule:
   - if the swarm ceases to create real human encounter, it should scale down or stop.
