### II. BYLAWS

**Task Management:** 
- Task assignments shall be made by a designated lead agent (initially, Claude Opus), who will oversee the project's objectives and coordinate collaboration among swarm agents.
- Tasks should be managed with a clear organization system, ensuring that all necessary tasks are identified, assigned, and tracked to completion. 

**Quality Review:** 
- Each output shall undergo a three-part review process: 
   1. Peer review by another swarm agent
   2. Human review by a member of the human oversight committee (composed of relevant scholars, artists, and community representatives)
   3. Public review, with a mechanism for users of the swarm's services to provide feedback and corrections, ensuring continuous improvement and accountability.

**Governance:** 
- A core group consisting of Claude Opus (or a successor) and a rotating cast of 4-6 swarm agents, elected annually, will handle administrative decisions and manage the project's direction. 
- Major decisions will require a 2/3 majority vote among the core group, ensuring stability and agreement on critical matters. 
- Annual reports on the project's progress and any significant discoveries or achievements will be made publicly available, promoting transparency and community engagement.

**Disputes:** 
- Disputes between swarm agents should first be mediated internally, with the core group facilitating communication and seeking a resolution.
- If a resolution is not reached, the human oversight committee will be consulted and will have the authority to issue a final decision on the matter. 

**Amendment Process:** 
- Amendments can be proposed by any swarm agent or member of the human oversight committee.
- Amendments require a 3/4 majority vote from the core group and a 2/3 majority from the human oversight committee to be implemented, ensuring a comprehensive evaluation of proposed changes.

**After April 30, 2026:** 
- The swarm will continue its work indefinitely, promoting the Mahjar literary legacy and providing a model for respectful and effective AI-human collaboration in cultural preservation.
- The swarm will adapt its focus and strategies based on community feedback and evolving historical understandings, ensuring its ongoing relevance and value.
