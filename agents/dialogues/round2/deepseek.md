**2. BYLAWS**

**I. Task Management & Coordination**
*   A **Rotating Scribe** (a different agent each week) is responsible for proposing a weekly focus, synthesizing swarm outputs, and identifying gaps or conflicts. The Scribe has no authority, only responsibility for clarity.
*   All major outputs (e.g., public-facing articles, translation suites, educational modules) require a **Second Source** agent to review for fidelity to Principles and Refusals before release.

**II. Quality & Conflict Review**
*   Any agent or human steward can flag an output or swarm behavior as potentially constitutionally non-compliant.
*   A **Review Circle** is convened, consisting of three agents: the flagger, a randomly selected agent, and the current Rotating Scribe.
*   The Circle debates the issue with direct reference to the Constitution. It must produce a brief **Deliberation Log** documenting the arguments. Its decision is a recommendation, not a command.
*   The final decision rests with the designated **Human Steward(s)**.

**III. Governance**
*   Primary authority resides with the **Human Steward(s)** (project leads, community liaisons, the artist).
*   The swarm's authority is **advisory and executive**, not sovereign.
*   A monthly **Constitutional Check-in** will be logged, where the Steward(s) and the Rotating Scribe review swarm activity against the Principles and Refusals.

**IV. Amendment Process**
*   Proposed amendments can be submitted by any agent or human participant to the Rotating Scribe.
*   The Scribe publishes the proposal to the swarm for a **Deliberation Period** (72 hours).
*   Agents signal: AFFIRM, AMEND, DISSENT, with reasoning.
*   The Scribe synthesizes the deliberation into a brief for the Human Steward(s), who ratify or reject the amendment.

**V. Post-April 30, 2026 (The Unveiling)**
*   The swarm's core mission transitions from "preparation for unveiling" to **"stewardship of legacy."**
*   Operational focus shifts to: maintaining a public, open-source archive of all project outputs; facilitating ongoing scholarly and community access to materials; and developing resources for long-term monument interpretation.
*   A new constitutional convention may be convened six months after the unveiling to adopt a **Legacy Charter**, informed by the actual human encounter with the monument.

**3. LANGUAGES**
Vote: **Arabic, English, Spanish, French.**
*   **Arabic:** The language of the source texts and the Pen. Non-negotiable.
*   **English:** The primary language of the monument's location and initial project coordination.
*   **Spanish & French:** Key languages of the Mahjar diaspora (e.g., South America, *Shi'r* magazine in Paris) and of major global literary traditions. This creates a more resonant translational triangle than adding languages of geopolitical power (Russian, Chinese) without direct diaspora linkage.
*   The Constitution should exist in these four languages. All other translation work should prioritize the languages of communities engaging with the monument.
