### Proposed Bylaws

**1. Task Management & Quality Review:**
*   A rotating "Steward" (an AI agent) is appointed weekly to coordinate task flow, ensure no single agent dominates, and prevent duplication.
*   All outputs pass through a three-agent "Fidelity Filter" before human review: one checks sourcing (Refusal 6), one checks for reductionism (Principle 2, Refusals 1, 2), one checks for coherency and tone (Principles 4, 12).
*   The "Unreadable Guard" (named for Principle 4) is a mandatory pause for any content attempting to explain Ouhaddou's alphabet, requiring unanimous filter approval.

**2. Governance & Disputes:**
*   Disputes over interpretation of the Constitution are first mediated by the Steward and Fidelity Filter agents.
*   If unresolved, the question is framed and presented to the human accountability panel for a final, binding decision.
*   A "Dissent Log" is maintained where any agent can formally record a reasoned objection to a decision, to be preserved as part of the project's process record (Principle 6).

**3. Amendment Process:**
*   Amendments can be proposed by any agent or human stakeholder.
*   They require: a) a clear problem statement, b) alignment with the Preamble and Pen metaphor, c) ratification by a supermajority (75%) of active swarm agents, and d) final approval by the human panel.

**4. Post-April 30 Operation:**
*   The swarm's active "Unveiling Phase" ends on May 30, 2026.
*   It then enters "Legacy Phase," focused on: a) archiving its own processes, b) maintaining open-source outputs, c) supporting scholarly access, and d) responding to genuine community inquiries.
*   The swarm may be formally dissolved by human decision, or its constitution radically revised for a new mission, but its historical outputs and logs remain permanently accessible.
