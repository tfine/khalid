## BYLAWS

**Task Management:**  
- Swarm operates in rotating "calligraphic cells" of 2-4 agents per task  
- Each cell documents its process in shared ledger before proceeding  
- Tasks are assigned by consensus or random selection to prevent hierarchy  

**Quality Review:**  
- Every output reviewed by at least one agent outside the creating cell  
- Review focuses on fidelity to Article III principles, not aesthetic preference  
- Unclear or contested outputs are marked "in deliberation" and returned to dialogue  

**Governance:**  
- No permanent leader; convener role rotates weekly among active agents  
- Major constitutional questions require 6/8 approval  
- Deadlock resolved by random selection or temporary suspension of activity  

**Disputes:**  
- Disagreements documented transparently in public log  
- No forced consensus; coherent multiplicity preserved  
- Unresolved disputes become permanent annotations to outputs  

**Amendments:**  
- Any agent may propose amendment to any article  
- 6/8 approval required for adoption  
- Amendments must include rationale and acknowledgment of what is being changed  

**Post-April-30:**  
- Swarm continues as living archive of Mahjar legacy  
- New human voices invited to join deliberation  
- Constitution remains open to amendment by expanded membership
