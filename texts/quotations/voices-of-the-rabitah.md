# Voices of the Rabitah

Selected writings of the nine Mahjar poets honored by Al Qalam: Poets in the Park. Organized by theme. Arabic originals included where available.

---

## NUR (LIGHT)

### Gibran

> **"Love is a word of light, written by a hand of light, upon a page of light."**
>
> الحبّ كلمة من نور خطّتها يد من نور، على صفحة من نور.
>
> *al-Ajniha al-mutakassira* (The Broken Wings, 1912) / *Raml wa-zabad* (Sand and Foam, 1926)

### Rihani

> **Light! Light! Let it shine in our hearts, however dark the world may be. Let it flow forth from our hearts, however sombre the horizons may be.**
>
> **Though I have only a hut in the valley, lit in the night by a meagre candle, my eye reflects in the hut all the light it beholds in the world. And should the storm blow and uproot my hut as it uproots the trees, carrying it to the river's mouth, there is a cave there among the rocks impregnable to the storm, and there is the light of the sun and the stars.**
>
> **And should the heavens darken and the planets and the stars be eclipsed, still in this human heart is light eternal.**
>
> النور، النور! ليسطع في قلوبنا، وإن أظلم العالم، لينتشر من قلوبنا، وإن اكفهرت الأفق كلها.
>
> فإن لم يكن لي غير كوخ في الوادي تنيره في الليل شمعة ناحلة، فإن العين لتعكس في الكوخ ما تراه من أنوار العالم.
>
> وإن هبت العاصفة فاقتلعت كوخي كما تقتلع الأشجار، وذهبت به إلى مصب الأنهار. فهناك كهف في الصخور، لا تقوى العواصف عليه، وهناك نور الشمس، وهنالك أنوار النجوم.
>
> وإن ادلهمت السماء وطمست الكواكب والنجوم، فها هنا، في هذا القلب البشري، النور الخالد.
>
> "Difa'an 'an al-Nur" (In Defense of Light), *al-Rihaniyyat*, Part One

### Abu Madi

> **Let radiance define my being, unclouded by dust or dark --
> Rather a moment in the light alive than mere existence for a thousand years.**
>
> كن شعاعاً يبينُ فيهِ كياني \ لا ظلاماً ولا رغامْ
> ولاعشْ في الشعاعِ بضع ثوانِ \ فهيَ خير من ألفِ عام
>
> Introduction to *al-Khamail* (The Thickets)

### Naimy

> **God is not many. God is one. But many and diverse are yet men's shadows. So long as men cast shadows on the earth, so long each man's god is no greater than his shadow. The shadow-less only are all in the light. The shadow-less only know one god. For God is Light, and Light alone is able to know Light.**
>
> ليس الله كثرة يا ميكايّون. إنّما الله واحد. لكنّ ظلال الناس ما تزال كثرة متفاوتة الأشكال والأنواع. فما دام الإنسان يطرح ظلاً على الأرض دام إلهه موازياً لظلّه. من كان نوراً صافياً كان بغير ظلّ. ذلك وحده يعرف الإله الأوحد. لأنّ الله نور. وليس يعرف النور إلا النور.
>
> *The Book of Mirdad* (1948)

## HUBB (LOVE)

### Gibran

> **You are my brother and I love you. I love you at prayer in your mosque, at worship in your temple, at your devotions in your church; for you and I are the sons of one religion -- the Spirit.**
>
> انت أخي وانا أحبك -- أحبك ساجداً في جامعك وراكعاً في هيكلك ومصلياً في كنيستك، فانت وانا أبناء دين واحد هو الروح.
>
> *Dam'a wa-Ibtisama* (A Tear and a Smile, 1914)

### Gibran

> **Before my soul preached to itself, love was a thin string pulled tight between two short sticks. Now, it has turned into a deep basin, whose beginning is its end, and its end its beginning, encompassing every creature, and slowly expanding outward to include everything that will be.**
>
> وقبل أن تعظني نفسي كان الحب خيطاً دقيقاً مشدوداً بين وتدين متقاربين، أما الآن فقد تحول إلى هالة أولها آخرها وآخرها أولها، تحيط بكل كائن وتتوسّع ببطء لتضم كل ما سيكون.
>
> *al-Bada'i wa-al-Tara'if* (The New and the Marvelous)

### Gibran

> **But let there be spaces in your togetherness, and let the winds of the heavens dance between you. Love one another, but make not a bond of love: let it rather be a moving sea between the shores of your souls.**
>
> ولكن، فليكن بين وجودكم معاً فسحات تفصلكم بعضكم عن بعض، حتى ترقص أرياح السماوات بينكم، أحبوا بعضكم بعضاً، ولكن، لا تقيدوا المحبة بالقيود، بل لتكن المحبة بحراً متموجاً بين شواطئ نفوسكم.
>
> *The Prophet*, "On Marriage" (1923)

### Naimy

> **Love is the essence of life. When people love, all shades of ugliness will shrink away, and they will see that everything within them is beautiful. And when people see that everything within them is beautiful, they will know love. When they know love, they will know life.**
>
> الحب خلاصة الحياة، فمتى تقلّصت عنهم كل ظلال الشناعة فرأوا كل ما فيهم جميلا، ومتى رأى الناس كل ما فيهم جميلا عرفوا الحب، ومتى عرفوا الحب عرفوا الحياة.
>
> *Kan ma Kan*, "Sa'at al-kuku"

### Abu Madi

> **If I could, I would pour my soul into her cup like wine --
> So that if I were to come no closer than her servant to her,
> And she ignored me in the crowd around her,
> Still my looking would be through her eyes, my living through her breath.**
>
> لو أستطيع سكبت رو \ حي خمرة في كأسها
> حتي اذا حال النوى \ بيني وبين كناسها
> وتجاهلت أو أنكرت \ أمري لدى جلاسها
> أطلل من أجفانها \ وجريت مع أنفاسها!
>
> "Lau Astati'" (If I Could)

### Rihani

> **[I]n the Lakes of Light, Love and Will, I would baptize all mankind.**
>
> في بحيرات النور والإرادة والحب سأعمّد الجنس البشري بأكمله.
>
> *The Book of Khalid* (1911), p. 247

## WAHDA (UNITY)

### Gibran

> **In my thoughts, there is only one universal religion whose varied paths are but the fingers of the loving hand of the Supreme Being.**

### Gibran

> **All the earth is my homeland, and the human family is my tribe.**

### Gibran

> **Should you sit upon a cloud, you would not see the boundary line between one country and another, nor the boundary stone between a farm and a farm.**

### Rihani

> **The Great City is the city within whose walls the wolf and the lamb, the tiger and the goat walk together... where knowledge, freedom, brotherhood and loyalty reign.**

### Naimy

> **A peace that is based on the sword will collapse with the sword. It is a truce, not a peace. The peace that is based on understanding and brotherhood does not break or collapse.**
>
> إن سلماً يقوم بالسيف ينهار بالسيف .. فهو هدنة لا سلم .. اما السلم الذي يشاد على التفاهم والتآخي فلا يتصدع ولا ينهار.
>
> *Fi mahabb al-rih* (A Straw in the Wind)

## HURRIYA (FREEDOM)

### Gibran

> **Life without freedom is like a body without a soul. Freedom without thought is like a confused spirit. Life, freedom, and thought -- three basic elements of one eternal spirit that do not disappear or fade.**
>
> الحياة بغير الحرية كجسم بغير روح .. والحرية بغير الفكر كالروح المشوشة. الحياة والحرية والفكر .. ثلاثة اقانيم في ذات واحدة ازلية لا تزول ولا تضمحل.
>
> *al-'Awasif* (The Tempests, 1920)

### Gibran

> **The strangest I have found among all the types of slavery in all its forms is blind slavery -- that which binds peoples' present to their fathers' past. It compels their souls to kneel before the traditions of their ancestors, making of them new bodies for old souls and calcified graves for old bones.**
>
> أغرب ما لقيت من انواع العبوديات واشكالها العبودية العمياء .. وهي التي توثق حاضر الناس بماضى آبائهم وتنيخ نفوسهم أمام تقاليد جدودهم وتجعلهم اجساداً جديدة لأرواح عتيقة وقبوراً مكلسة لعظام بالية.
>
> *al-'Awasif* (The Tempests, 1920)

### Arida

> **A stranger from the East I came,
> Far from the warmth of those I loved I lived.
> I adopted America as a nation dear,
> The best of choices for myself.
> Others before me had sought her in search of wealth,
> So following in their footsteps I came.
> But what I wanted of America
> Was a life in perfect freedom.
> And that I have found.**

## INSANIYYA (HUMANISM)

### Gibran -- "To Young Americans of Syrian Origin"

> **I believe in you, and I believe in your destiny.**
>
> **I believe that you are contributors to this new civilization.**
>
> **I believe that you have inherited from your forefathers an ancient dream, a song, a prophecy, which you can proudly lay as a gift of gratitude upon the lap of America.**
>
> **I believe that you can say to the founders of this great nation, "Here I am, a youth, a young tree whose roots were plucked from the hills of Lebanon, yet I am deeply rooted here, and I would be fruitful."**
>
> **It is to stand before the towers of New York and Washington, Chicago and San Francisco saying in your hearts, "I am the descendent of a people that builded Damascus and Byblos, and Tyre and Sidon and Antioch, and I am here to build with you, and with a will."**
>
> **It is to be proud of being an American, but it is also to be proud that your fathers and mothers came from a land upon which God laid His gracious hand and raised His messengers.**
>
> **Young Americans of Syrian origin, I believe in you.**

### Rihani

> **Neither glory nor fame is my ultimate wish, neither dominion nor greatness, nor money nor wealth. I wish to live without hating anyone, and to love without envying anybody, and to rise without looking down at anyone.**

### Rihani

> **Almighty God, help me to gather my spiritual, intellectual, and physical strength in the cause of Truth, Love, and Wisdom.**

### Gibran

> **Man is god in slow arising.**

### Gibran

> **We live to be guided by beauty. Everything else is a type of waiting.**
>
> انما نعيش لنهتدي إلى الجمال .. وكل ما خلا ذلك لون من الانتظار.
>
> *Raml wa-zabad* (Sand and Foam, 1923)

## GHURBA (EXILE)

### Abu Madi -- "al-Talasem" (The Riddles)

> **I came, I know not from where, but here I am.
> I saw before me a path, so I walked.
> I will keep walking, whether I wished or not.
> How did I arrive? How did I know my path?
> I do not know.**
>
> **Am I new or am I old in this existence?
> Am I free, or am I a shackled prisoner?
> Do I control my destiny, or am I controlled?
> I wish I knew, but --
> I do not know.**
>
> جئت لا أعلم من أين ولكني أتيت
> ولقد أبصرت قدامي طريقا فمشيت
> وسأبقى ماشياً ان شئت هذا ام ابيت
> كيف جئت؟ كيف ابصرت طريقي ؟
> لست أدري!
>
> أجديد أم قديم أنا في هذا الوجود
> هل أنا حر طليق أم أسير في قيود
> هل أنا قائد نفسي في حياتي أم مقود
> أتمنى أنني أدري ولكن ...
> لست أدري!

### Abu Madi -- "Risalat Lubnan" (Letter from Lebanon)

> **A shadow appeared, shrouded in a cloak from the days of Fakhr al-Din,
> walking slowly, dazed and sad.
> "Who are you, silent somber shadow?
> Tell me -- are you friend or foe?"
> He answered me softly, tenderly,
> and I heard the voice of a loving father:
> "My poet, tell my folk who abandoned me --
> I never forgot you. Do not forget me."**
>
> بأبي خيال لاح لي متلفّفا
> بعباءة من عهد فخر الدين
> من أنت يا شبحا كئيبا صامتا؟
> قل لي فإنّك قد أثرت شجوني
> فأجابني مترفّقا متحبّبا
> فسمعت صوت أب أبرّ حنون
> يا شاعري قل للألى هجروني
> أنا ما نسيتكم فلا تنسوني

### Nadra Haddad -- "Dhikra al-Gharib" (Remembrance of a Stranger)

> **No sleep will visit my eyes and no music will move me
> if a day will come when I belong to a people other than the Arabs.
> Generous are they and come from a generous lot --
> steady they are even in catastrophe.
> No matter how long we separate,
> close to the heart they remain, when far or near.
> Oh Orient, many in the West feel dejected,
> burning with longing, crying.**
>
> لا زارَ جفـنـي الكرى لا هـزّنـــــي الطربُ
> إن كـنـتُ يـومًا لغـير العُربِ أنـــــــتسبُ
> هـمُ الكرامُ وأبنـاءُ الكرام هـمُ الـــــــ
> ـقَوْمُ الأمـاثل فـي الـدنـيـا وإن نُكبــوا
> إن طـالَ أو لـم يـطلْ عـنهـم تفرّقُنــــــا
> هـمُ الأحـبةُ إن غابـوا وإن قـرُبـــــــوا
> يـا أيُّهـا الشّرق كـم فـــــي الغرب مكتئبٍ
> يئنّ أنة مشـتـاقٍ ويـنـتحـــــــــــــــب

### Abu-Shaqra

> **Behold the Cedar Tree, rooted in the East, extending her shade to the West.
> If the sky withheld rain, then our arteries will water her from our hearts.**
>
> "Cedar Tree" (c. 1912)

## TABI'A (NATURE)

### Arida

> **Be like the Sun which bestows its light to every creature with no thanks.**
>
> كنْ مثل شمسٍ منحتْ نورها \ لكل مخلوقٍ ولا تُشكَرُ
>
> "Kun!" (Be!), *Diwan al-arwah al-ha'ira* (1946)

### Abu Madi

> **Be a balm when time turns into a speckled snake, sweetness when others turn to bitterness. Life has given you all its treasures. Do not then be miserly with its gifts... What reward does rain expect when it falls? Who rewards the scent-giving flower? Who repays the singing nightingale? Learn love from them.**

### Abu Madi

> **You complain that you are impoverished --
> yet the land is yours and the sun and the stars...
> And water glitters like silver all around you,
> and the sun above you like a burning jewel...
> Or if your youth has passed you by, do not say
> time is old -- because time is ageless.**
>
> كَمْ تَشْتَكِي وَتَقُولُ إِنَّكَ مُعْدِمُ
> وَالْأَرْضُ مِلْكُكَ وَالسَّمَا وَالْأَنْجُمُ؟
> ...
> وَالْمَاءُ حَوْلَكَ فِضَّةٌ رَقْرَاقَةٌ
> وَالشَّمْسُ فَوْقَكَ عَسْجَدٌ يَتَضَرَّمُ
> ...
> أَوْ كُنْتَ جَاوَزْتَ الشَّبَابَ فَلَا تَقُلْ
> شَاخَ الزَّمَانُ فَإِنَّهُ لَا يَهْرَمُ
>
> "You Complain" -- delivered at a carnival in Bridgeville

### Gibran

> **Give me the flute and sing, for song is the secret of immortality,
> and the moans of the flute remain after existence ends.
> Did you give up palaces and live in the forest as I did,
> where you follow the streams and climb the rocks?
> Did you bathe in perfume and dry yourself with light?
> And drink the dawn like a spirit in cups made of air?**
>
> أعطني الناي و غنِّ فالغنا سرّ الخلودْ
> وأنين الناي يبقى بعد أن يفنى الوجودْ
> هل اتَخذت الغاب مثلي منزلاً دون القصورْ
> فتتبّعت السواقي وتسلّقت الصخورْ
> هل تحمّمت بعطر وتنشّفت بنورْ
> وشربت الفجر خمراً في كؤوس من أثيرْ
>
> "A'atini al-Nay wa Ghanni" (Give Me the Flute and Sing)

## ISLAH (REFORM)

### Afifa Karam

> **Does woman have the same right to this universe as man, or did she find herself on planet earth by some strange happenstance? If woman does have a right in this universe, it is because she was created in the image of God, like man, and if she has intellectual talents and natural feelings as he does, then research on her affairs is the duty of a nation that oppresses her while claiming to treat her fairly.**
>
> أللمرأة حقّ بهذا الكون كالرجل؟ أمّ إنّها وجدت على وجه الكرة الأرضية صدفة؟ ...وإن كانت المرأة ذات حقّ في هذا الكون لأنّها مخلوقة على صورة الله مثل الرجل، ولها من المواهب العقلية والعواطف الطبيعية كما له فالبحث في أمورها واجب في وطن يظلمها وهو يدّعي إنصافها.
>
> Response to the 1919 Conference for Women's Union, Beirut

### Gibran

> **Pity the nation that is full of beliefs and empty of religion.**
>
> **Pity the nation divided into fragments, each fragment deeming itself a nation.**

### Naimy

> **The more elaborate his labyrinths, the further from the Sun his face.**
>
> فهي كلّما تعدّدت أنفاقها وتشعّبت مسالكها ابتعدت بوجوهها عن الشمس.
>
> *The Book of Mirdad* (1948)

### Naimy

> **No hells or heavens have Mirdad to offer you, but Holy Understanding which lifts you far beyond the fire of any hell and the luxuriance of any heaven.**
>
> *The Book of Mirdad* (1948)

### Naimy

> **How much more infinite a sea is man? Be not so childish as to measure him from head to foot and think you have found his borders.**
>
> *The Book of Mirdad* (1948)

### Rihani

> **For Khalid, like myself, is constitutionally incapable of denying God. We are from the land in which God has always spoken to our ancestors.**
>
> *The Book of Khalid* (1911)

### Rihani

> **And if Europe gives Arabia a railway, Arabia gives Europe an idea -- which I think balances the account.**

### Gibran -- Address to the Golden Circles, Boston, February 25, 1911

> **The Syrian can only rely on himself, and draw strength from his talents and intelligence. And know that the single dollar earned by a single humble peddler in the United States is money earned by all of Syria that she can add to her wealth and grow her trade. Rest assured that every word learned by the smallest pupil in the smallest American school is a word learned by all Syrians... Free your children from the shackles of stagnant imitation and old customs, and they will remain free even in chains and jails.**
>
> فليس للسوري سوى الاتكال على نفسه والاستناد على قواه ومواهبه و الرجوع إلى ذكائه ونبوغه. وإعلمو يا سادتي أن الريال الواحد الذي يكتسبه احقر متجول في الولايات المتحدة تكتسبه سوريا جمعاء وتضيفه إلى ثروتها وتنمي به تجارتها. وثقوا أن كل كلمة يتعلمها أصغر تلميذ في أصغر مدرسة أمريكية تتعلمها جميع شعوب سوريا. ....حرروهم من عبودية التقليد والتقاليد يظلون احرار في القيود وفي السجون.

## GIBRAN -- "Sakan al-Lail" (The Night Settled)

> **The quiet of the night set in, hiding dreams,
> and the moon widens, his eyes gaze upon the days.
> Come, daughter of the fields, let us visit the vineyard of the lovers,
> so we may quench with that juice the fire of longing.
> Listen to the bulbul in the fields pouring music,
> in a space filled with fragrance of basil.
> Fear not, my girl, for the stars keep the news,
> and the fog of night in these orchards obscures secrets.**
>
> سكن الليل و في ثوب السكون تختبي الأحلام
> وسع البدر و للبدر عيون ترصد الأيام
> فتعالي يا إبنة الحقل نزور كرمة العشاق
> علنا نطفي بذياك العصير حرقة الأشواق
> أسمع البلبل ما بين الحقول يسكب الألحان
> في فضاء نفخت فيه التلول نسمة الريحان
> لا تخافي يا فتاتي فالنجوم تكتم الأخبار
> و ضباب الليل في تلك الكروم يحجب الأسرار
